# CareCompass — Design Guidelines

## Design authority
Mobile-first, touch-first. Cross-platform mobile (Samsung Android + iPhone 12) is the design authority; desktop/tablet is a bonus. One-handed use: thumb-friendly bottom navigation, large tap targets (min 44px), key info visible immediately without scrolling.

## Personality
Warm, supportive, calm, encouraging. Soft and human — never clinical or bureaucratic. Reduces overwhelm; frames progress positively.

## Color
- **Primary** — a calm teal/sage green (`#3F8E7C` family): trust, health, calm. Used for primary actions and active nav.
- **Secondary / warm accent** — a soft warm coral/amber (`#E8A87C` family) for encouragement, highlights, and "ready" moments.
- **Surface** — soft off-white / warm neutral background (`#FAF8F5`), white cards with gentle shadows.
- **Text** — deep warm charcoal (`#2E2A26`) for primary, muted warm grey for secondary.
- **Category accents** (calendar colour-coding): medical = teal, pharmacy = violet, wheelchair = amber, vehicle = blue. Used as soft pills/left-borders, never harsh.
- **Status** — success green, gentle warning amber, soft alert coral. Avoid aggressive red.

## Typography
- Friendly, highly legible humanist sans-serif (e.g. Inter / system UI). Generous sizes for mobile readability.
- Clear hierarchy: large warm greeting on the dashboard, bold section headers, comfortable body, generous line height.

## Layout & components
- Bottom tab navigation (5 tabs max) with large icons + labels; primary actions reachable by thumb.
- Cards with rounded corners (12–16px radius) and soft, low shadows. Generous padding and whitespace.
- Category pills, status chips, timeline list for the day. Empty states are warm and encouraging.
- One screen = one clear job. Avoid dense clinical tables.

## Elevation & motion
- Gentle elevation: soft diffuse shadows, no hard borders. Calm, subtle transitions; nothing jarring.

## Tone of copy
Affirming and human. "You're all set for today", "Two things to keep an eye on" — never "0 tasks pending" or clinical phrasing.
